Route 66

Route 66, often called the "Main Street of America," is one of the most famous highways in the United States. Stretching over 2,400 miles from Chicago, Illinois, to Santa Monica, California, it passes through eight states and numerous well-known cities. Starting in Chicago, it travels through St. Louis, Missouri, a city famous for its Gateway Arch. It continues to Oklahoma City, Oklahoma, known for its cowboy culture, and then heads to Amarillo, Texas, where you can see the famous Cadillac Ranch. The route also takes you through Albuquerque, New Mexico, and Flagstaff, Arizona, before reaching Los Angeles, California, and finally ending in Santa Monica, where it meets the Pacific Ocean. This historic highway is a journey through the heart of America, offering diverse landscapes and a glimpse of classic Americana.
